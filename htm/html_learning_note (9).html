3.3 Quotations and Citations

This chapter explains semantic quotation elements that help structure academic, literary, and technical writing.

Blockquotes (<blockquote>)

Executive Summary

Represents extended quotations. Browsers typically indent blockquotes.

Syntax & Example 
<blockquote>
  The only limit to our realization of tomorrow is our doubts of today.
</blockquote>
Deep Explanation

Blockquotes should be used for long passages, cited text, interviews, or research references.

Inline Quotes (<q>)

Executive Summary

<q> encloses short inline quotations. Browsers automatically add quotation marks.

Example 
<p>Einstein said, <q>Imagination is more important than knowledge</q>.</p>

Citations (<cite>)

Executive Summary

<cite> identifies the title of a creative work like a book, article, or research paper.

Example 
<p>This idea is explored in <cite>The Art of War</cite>.</p>
Deep Explanation

<cite> is semantic and should be used for titles, not author names.

Abbreviations (<abbr>)

Executive Summary

<abbr> provides full meaning for abbreviated terms using the title attribute.

Syntax & Example 
<p>
  The <abbr title="World Health Organization">WHO</abbr> is a UN agency.
</p>

Definitions (<dfn>)

Executive Summary

Defines a term at its point of introduction.

Syntax & Example 
<p>
  <dfn>Algorithm</dfn> is a step-by-step computational procedure.
</p>
